    LEBANESE ARAK

    Arak is a traditional alcoholic beverage of the Levant and Eastern Mediterranean and known as The Milk of Lions.

    Arak is a smooth, revitalizing spirit that clears the palate and wakes it for each new meal. It is known for its presence of anisette and licorice flavor with a dash of peppermint.

    Tradition dictates that arak is not consumed straight; Instead, it is mixed with water in a ratio of around 1/3 Arak to 2/3 water.

    Arak is often served with mezze, which may include dozens of small traditional dishes, as well as with grilled meat. It is also commonly served as an apritif, as it is known to aid with digestion and foster tranquility.
